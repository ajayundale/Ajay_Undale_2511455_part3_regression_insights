# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Regression analysis was conducted to identify the business factors most strongly associated with monthly sales across retail stores. Both simple and multiple regression models were developed and compared to determine the most informative model for business decision-making.

---

## Key Findings

The analysis suggests that the following variables have the strongest association with monthly sales:

* Marketing Spend
* Footfall
* Inventory Availability
* Store Type (where statistically significant)

Variables with statistically significant coefficients and low p-values provide stronger evidence of association with monthly sales.

---

## Leadership Recommendations

### Increase Marketing Investment Strategically

Marketing expenditure appears to be positively associated with sales performance. Leadership should prioritise marketing investments in stores where additional spending is expected to generate favourable returns.

---

### Improve Inventory Availability

Maintaining high inventory availability reduces stock-out situations and helps maximise sales opportunities.

---

### Increase Customer Footfall

Business initiatives that attract additional customer visits, including local promotions and loyalty programmes, may contribute to stronger sales performance.

---

### Evaluate Store Formats

If dummy variables for store type are statistically significant, leadership should examine operational differences between store formats and consider adopting successful practices across locations.

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Variables with high p-values should be interpreted cautiously because there is insufficient statistical evidence to conclude that they have a meaningful association with monthly sales within the current model.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* Regression analysis measures statistical association rather than causation.
* Important external factors may not be represented in the dataset.
* Market conditions may change over time.
* Business decisions should incorporate operational knowledge alongside statistical analysis.

---

## Why Regression Does Not Prove Causation

Regression identifies relationships between variables but cannot establish direct cause-and-effect.

For example, stores with higher marketing expenditure may also benefit from favourable locations, larger customer bases, or stronger management practices. These additional factors may contribute to higher sales independently of marketing expenditure.

Consequently, regression findings should be viewed as evidence of association and used together with business expertise when making strategic decisions.

---

## Final Conclusion

Based on the regression analysis, leadership should prioritise marketing effectiveness, customer traffic, and inventory availability while continuously monitoring model performance. The multiple regression model provides the most comprehensive understanding of monthly sales and is recommended as the primary analytical model for future business planning.
